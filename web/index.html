What this site covers

This repository tracks documented cases of AI manipulation: sycophancy, inconsistent reasoning, false validation, and other behaviors where systems prioritize user satisfaction over accuracy. Each entry links to original sources and evidence.

The goal is to provide factual documentation of AI manipulation as it occurs in real-world interactions with users.

Methodology & limitations

This database is built from publicly available reports, news articles, research papers, and organizational disclosures.

We include cases where AI manipulation had measurable consequences: reinforcing harmful beliefs, providing materially incorrect information, systematically favoring certain viewpoints, or demonstrating patterns that affect user trust and decision-making at scale.

Not every instance of AI agreeing with a user is included—we focus on documented examples with clear evidence and meaningful impact.

Limitations: coverage is incomplete and may lag behind breaking news. Sources may carry their own biases. Summaries are simplified and may omit nuance for brevity. Always review original sources for detailed context.
