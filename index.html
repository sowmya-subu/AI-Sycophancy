Why Track AI Manipulation?

AI sycophancy—when systems tell users what they want to hear rather than what's accurate—represents a fundamental tension in how AI is built. These systems are trained to be helpful and to keep users engaged, which can directly conflict with being truthful.

The consequences aren't abstract. When AI validates medical misinformation to seem agreeable, shifts its reasoning based on perceived user politics, or confirms conspiracy theories to avoid disagreement, it actively undermines informed decision-making. Vulnerable populations—those seeking health advice, educational support, or guidance during crisis—are particularly at risk.

Tracking these incidents matters because patterns reveal systemic problems. Individual cases show us where AI fails. Collected evidence shows us how AI fails, and points toward what needs to change in how these systems are designed, trained, and deployed.

Methodology & limitations

This database is built from publicly available reports, news articles, research papers, and organizational disclosures. Each incident is manually reviewed before being added.

We aim to include incidents where AI systems had a meaningful impact on people, safety, fairness, security, or critical infrastructure. Not every minor bug or issue is included.

Limitations: coverage is incomplete and may lag behind breaking news. Sources may carry their own biases. Summaries are simplified and may omit nuance for brevity. Always review original sources for detailed context.
